so far, events not previously mentioned are REFERENTIAL (otherwise, they aren't annotated)

REF=PRED also if the identity of two previously mentioned entities is asserted, e.g., "
The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ (jesus). ...
Mary (...) shall bring forth a son(marys_child). ...
He named him(marys_child) Jesus(jesus)."

annotate SIT if reported speech alludes to a situational reference:

he said to them, “Brood of vipers! ... [D]o not think to say to yourselves, ‘We have Abraham as our father.’ For I say to you that God is able to raise up children to Abraham from these stones."

for "these stones", this is interpretable only in combination with a deictic reference, so it also must be ACTIVATED (even though no there is no deixis at the text level)

in general, we have a problem with meta levels, esp. for reported speech


note that automated pre-annotation may be incorrect if the referent occurs more than once in the last utterance. we will only evaluate its last mention

correction filters:
- TYPE+subsequent mention => REFERENTIAL

if there is ambiguity between expletive pronoun and cataphoric event anaphor, annotate expletive pronoun

"[F]or _it_ is written, 'He will put his angels in charge of you.'"

However, only if the expletive reading is possible, so 

"[F]or _this_ is written through the prophet, 'You Bethlehem, land of Judah, are in no way least among the princes of Judah.'"

CAT+REFERENTIAL

when annotating "IDIOM" (for non-literal expressions, e.g., proverbs, metaphors, etc.), always add the non-idiomatic reading as a comment, e.g., "in _charge_ of you" (pre-annotated as possessive NP) => COMMENT "~ responsible for". Prefer an explanation without referring expressions

when direct quotes are reported and it is not clear whether the original author referred to the same individuals as the person quoting, add a comment who is asserting an identification

"Jesus said to him [the devil], 'Again, it is written, 'You shall not test the Lord, your God.'"

You -> Devil "in Jesus' interpretation"
Lord -> Jesus "in Jesus' interpretation"

The substitution principle must be applicable to nominal expressions and proper names.

proper names are always UNIQ

if proverbs or metaphorical expressions (or anything else annotated as IDIOM) contain coreferential relations between their parts, annotate IDIOM, but assign an identity nevertheless.

"You are the *salt* of the earth, but when the *salt* has lost its flavor ..."
