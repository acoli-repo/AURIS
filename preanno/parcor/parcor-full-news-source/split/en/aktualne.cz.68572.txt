The track cyclist Kelemen lost in the first round of the sprint against the French star Bauge.
To advance to the 1/8 finals, the Czech cyclist will have to do well in the repechage heat.
Having qualified among the top eighteen for the sprint event at the Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro, the track cyclist Pavel Kelemen lost in the first round duel against the French star Grégory Baugé, the defending silver medallist from the games in London.
To advance to the 1/8 finals, he will have to do well in the repechage heat.
Of the 27 cyclists who started in this event, the top Czech track cyclist finished with the fourteenth-best
200m flying lap of 9.969 seconds.
In the round of 16 he was beaten by the French cyclist Baugé, who qualified in the fifth place and already has a bronze from the team sprint event in Rio.
While Kelemen, was trying to chase after the experienced Frenchman, he made sure to advance in the next round.
Jason Kenny, the defending champion from London, shone again with an excellent performance.
The twenty-eight year old Briton rode the third fastest 200m flying lap in history, winning the qualification in a new Olympic record of 9,551.
It was, therefore, relatively easy for him to defeat as expected Maximilian Levy, who qualified in the last place.
The GB men's pursuit quartet, which included Bradley Wiggins, has also done well as it finished the 4km race in a world record of 3:50.570 and advanced to the finals.
They have managed to improve their previous personal best (3:51.659), which they held for four years from the Olympic Games in London by more than one second.
