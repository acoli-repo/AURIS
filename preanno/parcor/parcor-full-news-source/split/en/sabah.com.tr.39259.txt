Earthquake of 7.2 magnitude in the Pacific Ocean
The United States Geological Survey (USGS) announced that the earthquake was recorded as having an epicenter 109 kilometers east of New Caledonia Island and 10 kilometers in depth of the sea.
After the earthquake, which did not cause any life and property loss, the tsunami warning given was canceled after a short time.
Earthquakes often occur in Vanuatu, which is in the seismic belt of the "Pacific Ring of Fire."
