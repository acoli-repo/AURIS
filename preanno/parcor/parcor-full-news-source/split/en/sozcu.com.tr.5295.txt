17-year-old Şamil Tayyar Bayrakçı was hospitalized after he dove headfirst from the cliff called "Eagle Hill" from a height of 25 meters into the sea in Antalya.
Swimming off the coast of Konyaaltı with his friends during the evening hours, Şamil Tayyar Bayrakçı then dove headfirst from the cliffs known as "Eagle Hill" from a height of 25 meters.
Professional swimmer Şamil Tayyar Bayrakçı had a whiplash injury from the impact of hitting the water.
Having difficulty moving in the sea, Bayrakçı was taken to the shore by his friends, who are professional swimmers like him.
Arriving at the incident scene upon the report, the health officers brought Şamil Tayyar Bayrakçı to Antalya Training and Research Hospital by ambulance.
It was noted that there is no risk of death for Bayrakçı, who was taken into treatment.
One of Bayrakçı's friends, who gave a statement to the police, said, "When he dove headfirst from Eagle Hill, he got a whiplash injury."
He said, "We could not have known that this would happen."
