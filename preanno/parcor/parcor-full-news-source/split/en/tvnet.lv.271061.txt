McCartney: presence of Yoko Ono jeopardized The Beatles.
Paul McCartney has admitted that the presence of Yoko Ono made the legendary band The Beatles feel jeopardized.
The 74 year old rock star ''maintains'' good relations with the widow of John Lennon, but back when she came to visit the band in the recording studio, McCartney was afraid of her, because she was the only woman in the company of the band members George Harrison, Ringo Starr, Lennon and McCartney.
When the singer was asked about the relationships between them, he told to Rolling Stone that "they are really good."
Back then we felt threatened.
She sat on the amplifier while we were recording.
The majority of the band could not stand it.
We managed, but not very well as we were stressed out.
We weren't sexists but girls usually did not come to the recording studio, they'd let us do our thing.
When John started to hang out with Yoko, she sat among the four of us, not in the sound engineer's room.
The author of the hit song Yellow Submarine also admitted that he now regularly communicates with Ringo, the former band member, and occasionally approves the work plans of the recording studio Apple, but in other terms The Beatles have ceased to exist.
When asked, whether he still meets the other remaining member of the legendary rock group, McCartney revealed to Rolling Stone: "not very often".
We meet at social events and parties.
I do not go to meetings, I feel somehow disassociated from that.
I left Apple during my complicated divorce.
I sent John Eastman to represent me and told, "Tell me what they're saying, because I cannot stand sitting at that table".
It was too painful as if watching your pet die.
The company now works in such a manner that I have to only listen to the records.
I am a part of the approval process.
