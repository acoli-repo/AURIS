Great Britain's track cycling team breaks the Olympic record twice and wins in sprint.
The British track cycling team broke the Olympic record twice on Thursday and won the match sprint for men.
The British cyclists broke the first of the records in qualification and then it was broken again in the first round of the race by New Zealand athletes.
The Great Britain and New Zealand teams were racing in the final and the British athletes took away the title of Olympic record holder from New Zealanders, leaving them with silver medals.
The British team won the final with the result of 42.440 seconds outscoring the New Zealand's team by 0.102 seconds.

Additionally, a record was broken also in individual time trial qualification for women: the British team broke the world record, currently standing at 4:13.260.
