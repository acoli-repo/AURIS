He built an island for himself
The famous architect, Richart Sowa, spent the last 8 years of his life building a floating ecological haven.
He is living with the former model Jodi Bowlin, whom he met on Facebook two years ago, in this self-contained living space that he built using 150 thousand plastic bottles.
Richart's island is made of air-filled plastic bottles, wooden pallets, and sand.
In the two story house, there are 2 baths, a jacuzzi, 3 shower cubicles, a kitchen and a wireless internet connection.
There are palm trees, mangroves, fruit trees, other edible plants and grass around the island.
Linked to the coast by a 30-meter-long cable, the island produces its own electricity, water and internet with the help of this cable.
Sowa said, "I am a vegetarian and grow many edible vegetables on the island."
I need to go to the other small markets in the area to obtain other food.
He said, "Also, I have a raft for 8 people which I made from the plastic bottles."
