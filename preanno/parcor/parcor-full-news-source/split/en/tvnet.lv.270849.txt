Oil price continues to rise; stock exchange indexes in USA and Europe and value of US dollar increase.
Oil prices continued to grow globally on Friday morning as Saudi Arabia oil minister's statements positively affected investors' mood, and it is expected that the largest oil mining countries will take action at the OPEC meeting next September to stabilize the global oil market.
The three main US stock exchange indexes hit a high on Thursday resembling that of 1999.
The main positive indicator in the market was an oil price increase and strong financial indicators of several big companies.
The oil price increase and strong financial indicators of several companies on Thursday encouraged the rise in European stock exchange indicators.
The value of the US dollar as compared to the euro, British pound and Japanese yen increased on Thursday.
The WTI crude oil price at the New York Stock Exchange electronic trading platform for supplies in September increased by 1.78 US dollars up to 43.49 dollars per barrel on Thursday.
The price of Brent crude oil for supplies in October increased by 1.99 dollars up to 46.04 dollars per barrel at the London Stock Exchange.
